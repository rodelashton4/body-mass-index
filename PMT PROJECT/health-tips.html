BMI Health Tips & Information

Understanding Your BMI Results

Your Body Mass Index (BMI) is a useful indicator of whether you're at a healthy weight for your height. Here's what the different BMI categories mean:

  • Underweight (BMI less than 18.5): You may need to gain weight for optimal health. Consult with a healthcare provider to develop a healthy weight gain plan.
  • Normal weight (BMI 18.5-24.9): Your weight is in a healthy range for your height. Maintain your weight through balanced nutrition and regular physical activity.
  • Overweight (BMI 25-29.9): You may be at increased risk for certain health conditions. Consider lifestyle changes to improve your health.
  • Obese (BMI 30 or greater): You may be at higher risk for serious health problems. A healthcare provider can help you develop a weight management plan.

Remember that BMI is just one indicator of health. Other factors like muscle mass, body composition, and overall lifestyle also play important roles in your wellbeing.

Healthy Eating

Focus on whole foods like fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, and whole grains. Limit processed foods and added sugars for better health.

Regular Exercise

Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate exercise per week. Include both cardio and strength training for optimal results.

Hydration

Drink plenty of water throughout the day. Proper hydration supports metabolism and helps control hunger.

Quality Sleep

Get 7-9 hours of quality sleep each night. Poor sleep can affect hormones that regulate hunger and metabolism.
